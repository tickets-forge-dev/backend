## DETAILED SHOOTING ORDER

**Do NOT film in script order. Film by location & setup to save time.**

### SETUP PHASE (30 min)
1. Set up camera on tripod
2. Adjust lighting (window + ring light if needed)
3. Audio test (record 10 sec of PM speaking)
4. Monitor check (frame PM, white balance, exposure)
5. Have PM sit and get comfortable

### SHOOT PHASE (90 min)

**Block 1: All Desk Shots (70 min)**
- Scenes 1A-1D (Confusion setup): 10 min
- Scenes 2B-2C (Reaction shots): 10 min
- Scenes 3A & 3E & 5A-5C (All PM reactions): 20 min
- Scenes 2D montage (GitHub, Zoom, desk): 15 min
- Pick up shots / alternate takes: 15 min

**Block 2: Screen Recordings (20 min)**
- Pre-record Scene 3B-3F (Forge interface)
- This is done separately on a computer, not on set
- But discuss timing/flow while filming

### WRAP (10 min)
- Check all footage on camera
- Do 1-2 safety takes of key shots
- Backup footage immediately

## TECHNICAL SPECS FOR FILMING

### Camera Settings
```
Resolution: 4K (3840x2160) or 1080p (1920x1080)
Frame Rate: 24fps (cinematic) or 30fps (safe)
Shutter Speed: 1/48s (if available, 180-degree shutter)
ISO: Auto (or 400-1600 max depending on light)
White Balance: Auto or tungsten (adjust for lighting)
Focus: Auto or manual (lock focus on PM's eyes)
Codec: H.264 or ProRes (if capable)
```

### Audio Settings
```
Mic: Lapel/lavalier (wireless preferred, wired OK)
Levels: -12dB to -6dB (never peak above -3dB)
Sample Rate: 48kHz
Bit Depth: 16-bit (minimum)
Record backup audio on phone as safety
```

### Screen Recording Settings
```
Tool: ScreenFlow (Mac), Camtasia, or QuickTime
Resolution: 1920x1080 minimum
Frame Rate: 60fps (smoother motion)
Codec: H.264 or ProRes
Quality: Highest available
```

## RECORDING BACKUP: "EASY MODE"

If you want to film faster:

**Option A: Voiceover Everything**
- Film PM silent (just reactions, expressions)
- Record all dialogue separately as voiceover
- Much easier to capture authentic expressions

**Option B: Film Yourself**
- You play the PM (fastest, no actor needed)
- Dialogue is more natural
- Tripod + phone is all you need

**Option C: Use Stock Footage**
- Record PM reactions only (no face/name visible)
- Hire voice actor on Fiverr (~$50)
- Speeds up production, still authentic

## EDITING TIMELINE

### Day 1: Filming (2-3 hours)
- Film all scenes
- Backup footage to two drives
- Review footage same day

### Day 2: Editing (4-6 hours)

**Step 1: Organize Footage (30 min)**
- Import all video files
- Organize by scene (1, 2, 3, 4, 5)
- Label best takes

**Step 2: Cut Rough Assembly (90 min)**
- Assemble in script order
- Trim to 60 seconds total
- Focus on timing, not effects yet

**Step 3: Add Audio & Music (60 min)**
- Lapel mic audio synced to footage
- Add voiceover (if needed)
- Add sound effects:
  - Comedic fail horn (confusion section)
  - Satisfying "ding" (spec reveal)
  - Uplifting background music (Epidemic Sound or Artlist)

**Step 4: Visual Effects (60 min)**
- Color grade (Act I: desaturated/cool, Act II: warm/vibrant)
- Split-screen comparison (Scene 4)
- Text overlays (timing, quality scores, CTA)
- Transitions (cuts, wipes, fades)

**Step 5: Polish (60 min)**
- Fine-tune timing to music
- Fix audio levels (normalize to -14dB LUFS)
- Motion graphics for Forge logo/CTA
- Final review, adjust pacing

**Step 6: Export (15 min)**
- YouTube: MP4, H.264, 1080p@24fps or 4K@24fps
- Social: Square (1:1), 16:9, 9:16 versions
- Backup to drive

## CONTINGENCY PLANS

**If PM actor isn't available:**
- Film yourself (fastest)
- Hire actor on Fiverr for $50-100
- Use voiceover + silent PM reactions

**If lighting is bad:**
- Reschedule for different time of day (golden hour = best)
- Use ring light (even cheap one works)
- Film in overcast daylight (softest, most flattering)

**If audio is bad:**
- Record voiceover separately in a quiet room
- Add captions (required anyway for accessibility)
- Re-record any dialogue that's muffled

**If spec reveal is glitchy:**
